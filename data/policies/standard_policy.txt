# Saudi Health Insurance Policy - Standard Coverage Guidelines

## Document Version: 1.0
## Last Updated: November 2025
## Compliant with: CCHI and NPHIES Standards

---

## 1. Coverage Overview

This health insurance policy provides comprehensive medical coverage for members in accordance with the Council of Cooperative Health Insurance (CCHI) regulations and NPHIES requirements in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia.

### 1.1 Covered Services

The following services are covered under this policy:

#### Medical Consultations
- Routine medical consultations with general practitioners
- Specialist consultations (with referral)
- Emergency room visits
- Telemedicine consultations
- Annual preventive health checkups

#### Diagnostic Services
- Laboratory tests (blood tests, urinalysis, etc.)
- X-ray examinations
- Ultrasound imaging
- CT scans (with prior authorization)
- MRI scans (with prior authorization)
- PET scans (with prior authorization)
- Genetic testing (with prior authorization and medical necessity)

#### Surgical Procedures
- Medically necessary surgical procedures
- Emergency surgical interventions
- Day surgery procedures
- Inpatient surgical procedures
- Anesthesia services
- Post-operative care

#### Hospitalization
- Inpatient hospital stays
- Intensive care unit (ICU)
- Coronary care unit (CCU)
- Semi-private room accommodation
- Hospital supplies and medications
- Nursing care

#### Chronic Disease Management
- Diabetes management
- Hypertension management
- Asthma and respiratory conditions
- Cardiac conditions
- Oncology care
- Renal disease management

#### Maternity Care (if included in plan)
- Prenatal care
- Delivery (normal and cesarean)
- Postnatal care
- Complications of pregnancy

---

## 2. Excluded Services

The following services are NOT covered under this policy:

### 2.1 Cosmetic Procedures
- Cosmetic surgery
- Aesthetic procedures
- Dental cosmetic work
- Vision correction surgery (LASIK) for cosmetic purposes

### 2.2 Fertility Treatments
- In-vitro fertilization (IVF)
- Fertility medications
- Fertility testing and procedures
- Note: May be covered under special riders

### 2.3 Experimental Treatments
- Experimental or investigational procedures
- Clinical trial participation
- Unproven medical treatments
- Off-label medication use (unless medically justified)

### 2.4 Alternative Medicine
- Traditional medicine
- Herbal treatments
- Homeopathy
- Acupuncture (unless specifically covered)

### 2.5 Weight Management
- Weight loss surgery (unless medically necessary with BMI > 40)
- Weight loss programs
- Dietary supplements

## 3. Prior Authorization Requirements

### 3.1 Mandatory Prior Authorization

The following services REQUIRE prior authorization:

#### High-Cost Procedures
- Any surgical procedure exceeding 20,000 SAR
- Total joint replacements (hip, knee)
- Cardiac procedures (bypass, stenting, valve replacement)
- Neurosurgery
- Organ transplants

#### Advanced Imaging
- MRI scans
- CT scans
- PET scans
- Nuclear medicine studies

#### Specialty Medications
- Biologic agents
- Chemotherapy medications
- Medications exceeding 5,000 SAR per month
- Specialty injectable medications

#### Extended Therapies
- Physical therapy beyond 10 sessions
- Occupational therapy beyond 10 sessions
- Speech therapy beyond 10 sessions
- Rehabilitation programs

### 3.2 Authorization Process

To obtain prior authorization:
1. Submit request at least 5 business days before service
2. Include complete medical documentation
3. Provide clinical justification for medical necessity
4. Include recent diagnostic results
5. Specify expected costs and treatment plan

### 3.3 Emergency Services

Emergency services do NOT require prior authorization but must be reported within 24 hours.

---

## 4. Medical Necessity Criteria

### 4.1 Definition

A service is considered medically necessary when:
- Appropriate for diagnosis or treatment of the medical condition
- Consistent with accepted standards of medical practice
- Not primarily for convenience of patient or provider
- Not experimental or investigational
- Most appropriate and cost-effective option available

### 4.2 Documentation Requirements

Medical necessity must be supported by:
- Clinical notes from treating physician
- Relevant diagnostic test results
- Treatment history and previous interventions
- Evidence-based medical literature (if applicable)
- Second opinion (for complex cases)

### 4.3 Review Process

Medical necessity is evaluated by:
- Clinical review team
- Board-certified physicians in relevant specialty
- Evidence-based guidelines and protocols
- CCHI approved medical policies

## 5. Cost Sharing and Limits

### 5.1 Copayments

Standard copayments apply:
- General practitioner consultation: 100 SAR
- Specialist consultation: 150 SAR
- Emergency room visit: 300 SAR (waived if admitted)
- Diagnostic tests: 20% of cost
- Prescription medications: 15% of cost

### 5.2 Annual Limits

- Maximum annual benefit: 500,000 SAR per member
- Specific procedure limits:
  - Total joint replacement: 100,000 SAR per joint
  - Cardiac surgery: 150,000 SAR per procedure
  - Cancer treatment: 300,000 SAR per year
  - Dialysis: Unlimited (chronic condition)

### 5.3 Lifetime Limits

- No lifetime maximum for most conditions
- Organ transplants: 1 per member lifetime
- Certain congenital conditions: Subject to review

---

## 6. Network and Provider Requirements

### 6.1 In-Network Providers

- Coverage: 90% after copayment
- No prior referral needed for specialists
- Direct billing available
- Preferred provider network

### 6.2 Out-of-Network Providers

- Coverage: 70% after copayment
- Prior authorization required
- Member must pay difference between billed and approved amounts
- Maximum reimbursement limits apply

### 6.3 International Treatment

- Emergency treatment abroad: Covered up to 50,000 SAR
- Elective treatment abroad: Not covered
- Medical evacuation: Covered in emergencies

## 7. Age-Specific Coverage

### 7.1 Pediatric Care (0-14 years)

Enhanced coverage includes:
- All recommended vaccinations
- Growth and developmental monitoring
- Pediatric preventive care
- Congenital condition treatment

### 7.2 Adult Care (15-59 years)

Standard coverage includes:
- Routine health maintenance
- Chronic disease management
- Preventive screenings
- Reproductive health (for women)

### 7.3 Elderly Care (60+ years)

Enhanced coverage includes:
- Geriatric assessments
- Fall prevention programs
- Chronic disease management
- Home health services (with approval)

## 8. Orthopedic Services Specifics

### 8.1 Total Knee Replacement

**Coverage Criteria:**
- Conservative treatment failed for at least 6 months
- Documented osteoarthritis or joint damage
- Significant functional impairment
- Age consideration: Typically 50+ years
- BMI less than 40 (unless medically managed)

**Required Documentation:**
- Recent X-rays or MRI (within 6 months)
- Physical therapy records
- Pain management history
- Functional assessment
- Surgeon's clinical notes

**Pre-Authorization:** REQUIRED
**Expected Coverage:** 90% after copay
**Maximum Benefit:** 100,000 SAR

### 8.2 Hip Replacement

Similar criteria to knee replacement with:
- Evidence of avascular necrosis, fracture, or severe arthritis
- Failure of conservative management
- Appropriate surgical candidate

### 8.3 Spinal Surgery

**Requires:**
- 12 months conservative treatment
- MRI confirmation of pathology
- Neurological consultation
- Failed epidural steroid injections (if applicable)
- Second opinion for complex cases

## 9. Oncology Services

### 9.1 Cancer Treatment

Covered services include:
- Chemotherapy (inpatient and outpatient)
- Radiation therapy
- Surgical oncology
- Targeted therapy
- Immunotherapy
- Palliative care

### 9.2 Screening Programs

Covered preventive cancer screenings:
- Mammography: Annual for women 40+
- Colonoscopy: Every 10 years starting age 50
- PSA testing: Annual for men 50+
- Pap smear: Every 3 years for women 21-65

---

## 10. Cardiac Services

### 10.1 Diagnostic Cardiology

Covered services:
- Electrocardiogram (ECG)
- Echocardiogram
- Stress tests
- Cardiac catheterization (with medical necessity)
- Holter monitoring

### 10.2 Interventional Cardiology

Prior authorization required:
- Coronary angioplasty and stenting
- Cardiac valve procedures
- Pacemaker implantation
- Coronary artery bypass grafting (CABG)

**Medical Necessity Criteria:**
- Documented cardiac disease
- Failed medical management
- Appropriate surgical candidate
- Benefit outweighs risk

---

## 11. Appeals and Grievances

### 11.1 Appeal Rights

Members have the right to appeal:
- Denied authorizations
- Coverage determinations
- Medical necessity decisions

### 11.2 Appeal Process

1. **Level 1 - Internal Review**
   - Submit within 30 days of denial
   - Response within 15 business days
   - Clinical peer review

2. **Level 2 - External Review**
   - Submit within 30 days of Level 1 decision
   - Independent medical review
   - Response within 30 business days

### 11.3 Urgent Appeals

For urgent/emergency situations:
- Response within 72 hours
- Verbal notification permitted
- Written follow-up within 3 days

## 13. Policy Definitions

**Medical Necessity:** Treatment or service that is appropriate and necessary for diagnosis or treatment.

**Prior Authorization:** Advance approval required before receiving certain services.

**In-Network:** Healthcare providers contracted with the insurance company.

**Copayment:** Fixed amount paid by member at time of service.

**Annual Maximum:** Maximum amount insurance will pay per year.
